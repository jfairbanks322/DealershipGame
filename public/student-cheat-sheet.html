Student Guide

Stock Arena Cheat Sheet

Everything you need to log in, follow the market, buy and sell stocks, and keep track of your portfolio during class.

Market Open Student View
Starting Cash $10,000
Watchlist Up to 5
Goal Grow your total value

Your score is your cash plus the current value of all the shares you own.

1. Get In

Login and Setup

  1. Create your account with your display name, username, and password the first time you play.
  2. Use the same login each class so your portfolio and rank stay saved.
  3. Do not share your password unless your teacher resets it for you.
If you cannot get in: ask your teacher to reset your password. Your portfolio can stay the same even if the password changes.

2. Read The Screen

What You Are Looking At

Portfolio Value = your score Cash Ready = money not invested Trade Board = company research + trading Leaderboard = class ranking News Wire = teacher events
  • Green numbers usually mean a price went up.
  • Red numbers usually mean a price went down.
  • Your watchlist lets you save the companies you want to keep an eye on.

3. Trade

How To Buy and Sell

  1. Find a company using the company list, search box, or filters.
  2. Click the company to read its bio, pros, cons, industry, and current price.
  3. Enter the number of shares you want to buy or sell.
  4. Click Buy or Sell only while the market is open.
Important: if the market is closed, you can still research companies, but your trade buttons will not work.

4. Watchlist

Track Your Favorites

  • Click the star next to a company to add it to your watchlist.
  • You can watch up to 5 companies at a time.
  • Use the watchlist panel to jump back to those companies quickly during class.

5. Pay Attention

Teacher Events Matter

  • Read the News Wire because teacher events can change prices immediately.
  • One event can help some companies and hurt others at the same time.
  • Think before you panic. A bad event might be temporary, but a risky company can crash hard.

6. Play Smart

Quick Strategy Tips

  • Do not put everything into one stock unless you are comfortable with big risk.
  • Cheap stocks are not always better. Look at the company details, not just the price.
  • Stable companies may grow slowly, while risky companies can jump or crash fast.
  • Check your rank, but do not chase every move on the leaderboard.

Fast Reminder

Class-Day Routine

  1. Log in.
  2. Check your portfolio, rank, and watchlist.
  3. Read the latest event or news update.
  4. Research companies on the trade board.
  5. Trade before the market closes.
  6. Check how your choices changed your score.
